Interactive Media Assignment 1

Interactive Experience Research Project

Selected Website: THE MAN WHOSE WORK WE DON'T NOTICE

https://brooomstick.com/

Question 1.

What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The first thing I paid attention to when interacting with the experience was the opening animation that plays when the page is initially loading. The art style was very reminiscent of comic-book style dot printing. Upon completion of the page loading, the first thing I noticed was the main tag-line, “The man whose work we don’t notice”. Having a large title or tagline at the top of your page helps the viewer understand immediately what your webpage is about. You want the biggest element to be the first one seen.

Question 2.

Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

  1. I read the top navigation bar options, ‘PROFESSION, PAINTINGS, WHAT HAPPENS IF, WHAT CAN BE DONE’ and decided to start by scrolling down the page to read more.
  2. I scrolled down the page to see a few animations such as the janitor character moving from left to right, the broom in his hand swinging, and the images of trash disappearing as they moved through them.
  3. I then noticed that the background of the page wasn’t a solid colour background but actually an image of what looked like to be dark-coloured tissues or paper to suit the theme of the webpage.
  4. I noticed the drawing animation around the word, ‘BUT’ to symbolise the urgency around that word in the sentence
  5. I read the next paragraph and looked at the supporting imagery given
  6. After scrolling down more, I had paid more attention to the visual theme of the website. I noticed the images and text boxes were bordered with torn paper and that most of the background images are very reminiscent of paper cut-outs and cut comic book art as if they were scrap pieces of paper, as trash.
  7. I scrolled down to the, ‘BUT THAT’S CHANGED NOW’ section and saw the animation of the paper slips falling off of the flyers.
  8. I noticed the page sometimes would not always continue scrolling. Sometimes the page would stop scrolling down but instead would play animations that change as you scroll either up or down.

Question 3.

What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I spent most of the time interacting with the various animations on the page. I found myself very fascinated with how the creator was able to have the page pause in certain sections to play an animation rather than continue scrolling. While interacting with those particular sections, I found myself wondering how this was achieved and I estimated that the page actually was scrolling with a static background and the images were following the centre of your screen as you scroll.

Question 4.

What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

The most common action while I was interacting with this website was scrolling. Apart from clicking on the navigation buttons at the top of the page, the only other interactable element was the scrolling of the page. Scrolling was the only action that allowed the page to progress its story and message.

Question 5.

What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

My impression of the intended primary goal was to create awareness around the disrespect and ignorance we give to janitorial figures and other people in working professions in today’s society. The text highlighted how janitors were once highly regarded in the past and how we should treat them better today.

Question 6.

How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

This goal is communicated by both informative and historical text combined with simple 2D animations. The text was written for more in-depth explanations about janitors and their history, whereas the animations were included as supportive imagery and to potentially cater for younger audiences observing this webpage too.

Question 7.

What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

I think this experience only needs to be used once for about 2-4 minutes to understand the primary message. It took me 2 minutes and 40 seconds to scroll down to the bottom of the page reading each piece of text rather quickly and going through each animation once quickly. If someone were using this for the first time, I would estimate it would take them a little longer to take in every piece of information. As the experience is the same each time, there would not be a use case for trying it a second time.

Question 8.

How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

The interaction conveys how it should be interacted with by having animations that change as you scroll and having more information that appears as you scroll. It makes it obvious what to do by giving more as you repeat the same action.

Question 9.

What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

The experience references newspaper articles, film photography, and paintings through its supportive imagery and history about the janitor.

Question 10.

What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

The references to physical forms of media indicate that you should interact with the webpage in a physical manner of scrolling. Although there are navigation buttons at the top of the page, I estimate these are for accessibility’s sake. In physical forms of media such as a newspaper your eyes read down each article, similar to the act of scrolling on a webpage.

Question 11.

What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

The references to physical forms of media communicate you should feel that there is a rich history to janitors and working professionals and that it is ignorant of us to insult them.

Question 12.

What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

The most frustrating part of the interaction is when you are observing the animations, they never appear perfectly smooth as they rely on your scrolling speed to move. Each animation usually plays in about 3 quick scrolls which doesn’t look pleasing.

Question 13.

What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

The most satisfying part of the interaction is seeing the short marker animations around words appear. They draw attention to what they’re outlining and always appear smooth, no matter the scrolling speed.
